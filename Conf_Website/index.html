About Bayes Comp

Bayes Comp is a biennial conference sponsored by the ISBA section of the same name. The conference and the section both aim to promote original research into computational methods for inference and decision making and to encourage the use of frontier computational tools among practitioners, the development of adapted software, languages, platforms, and dedicated machines, and to translate and disseminate methods developed in other disciplines among statisticians.

Bayes Comp is current incarnation of the popular MCMSki series of conferences, and Bayes Comp 2018 is the first edition of this new conference series.

Location

The workshop will take place in the Ciutadella Campus of UPF (Roger de Llúria building; rooms 40.002 and 40.S02), which is less than 10 minutes walk from Barceloneta beach and from the picturesque El Born neighbourhood.

Bayes Comp will start on the morning of Monday 26th of March and finish in the evening of Wednesday 28th of March.

Travel Support

Submission of travel support applications starts December 15 2017 and ends January 15 2018. Awards will be notified as soon as possible after January 15 2018.

Eligibility criteria : support is for young researchers (students currently enrolled in an MSc/PhD program or a maximum of 3 years after graduation) who are already registered and whose poster/oral presentation has been approved.

Applicants should email David Rossell a one-page letter with basic CV info (place/date of academic qualifications) summarizing the research they will present. They must also email proof of current enrollment (letter from school or MSc/PhD certificate featuring the graduation date).



Accommodation

Code of Conduct

ISBA takes very seriously any form of misconduct, including but not limited to sexual harassment and bullying. An official ISBA code of conduct is expected to be released soon, all meeting participants are expected to adhere strictly to it and conduct themselves in a professional manner. Following the safeISBA motto, we want ISBA meetings to be safe and to be fun. We encourage participants to report any concerns or perceived misconduct to the meeting organizers, David Rosell and Piotr Zwiernik. Further suggestions can be sent to safeisba@bayesian.org.
